    Augury Grid v1.0 ‚Äî Multi-Symbol Screener¶

    Real-time scanner displaying MACD histogram crossovers with confluence filters across multiple assets simultaneously. Monitor up to 40 tickers from single dashboard view.

    üëã

    New to Trading? Start Here

    In Plain English: Instead of checking charts one-by-one to find signals, Augury Grid shows you 8-40 markets at once in a clean table format. Think of it like:

    • A radar screen showing all nearby aircraft
    • A security monitor showing all camera feeds
    • A sports scoreboard showing all games

    What You'll See:

    A grid/table showing multiple symbols with their current status:

    • Symbol Name (e.g., BTC, ETH, AAPL, SPY)
    • Signal Direction (‚Üë Bullish or ‚Üì Bearish)
    • Confluence Score (how many filters align)
    • Take Profit Level (TP1) and current P&L
    • Gauss Ribbon Status (trend filter)

    What Signals Does It Show?

    Augury Grid detects MACD histogram crossovers filtered by multiple confluence factors:

    • ‚Üë Bullish Signal = MACD histogram crosses above zero with confluence confirmation
    • ‚Üì Bearish Signal = MACD histogram crosses below zero with confluence confirmation
    • Confluence Filters: ADX (trend strength), Higher Timeframe alignment, OBV (volume), RSI (momentum), Distance from moving average
    • Score Display = Shows how many confluence factors align (higher = stronger signal)
    • ‚ö™ NO SIGNAL = MACD histogram hasn't crossed or confluence filters not met

    Perfect For:

    • Scanning multiple crypto pairs quickly
    • Finding the strongest setups across stocks
    • Monitoring your watchlist without clicking through charts
    • Spotting which markets are showing signals RIGHT NOW
    üéì What's MACD histogram?

    MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) is a momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages. The histogram shows the difference between the MACD line and its signal line:

    • Histogram crosses above zero = Bullish momentum developing
    • Histogram crosses below zero = Bearish momentum developing

    Augury Grid doesn't just show raw MACD crosses‚Äîit adds confluence filters (ADX, HTF, OBV, RSI, distance) to show only the highest-probability setups.

    You don't need to understand the technical details! Augury Grid analyzes all the confluence factors and shows you a simple score and direction for each symbol.

    Beginner Starting Point:

    1. Start with 4-8 symbols you're familiar with (don't overwhelm yourself with 40)
    2. Watch for ‚Üë Bullish or ‚Üì Bearish signals with high confluence scores
    3. Click any symbol in the grid to jump directly to that chart
    4. Use it as a filter‚Äîonly look at charts showing active MACD crossover signals

    üí° Pro Tip: Augury Grid is perfect for crypto traders monitoring 10+ pairs, or stock traders scanning their watchlist for MACD momentum shifts. It saves you from clicking through dozens of charts looking for setups.

    Approach:

    - Identify sector-wide momentum patterns

    Use Case: Sector-specific analysis and comparison

    Approach:

    Approach:
    - Track symbols in current portfolio holdings
    - Monitor for MACD momentum shifts on held positions
    - Use confluence scores as position management reference

    Use Case: Portfolio-level awareness of momentum changes

    Example Watchlist: Personal holdings (stocks, crypto, etc.)

    üìö Interpreting Grid Patterns¶

    Pattern 1: Broad Market Momentum¶

    Observation:
    Multiple symbols (5+) show bullish or bearish MACD signals with high confluence (4-5/5) simultaneously across same sector or asset class.

    Interpretation:
    Sector-wide or market-wide momentum shift in progress.

    Example: 8 out of 10 tech stocks showing bearish signals with 4-5/5 scores ‚Üí Broad tech sector momentum reversal characteristics.

    Pattern 2: Divergent Signals¶

    Observation:
    Some symbols show bullish signals while related symbols show bearish signals.

    Interpretation:
    Divergence in momentum direction within sector or asset class.

    Example: BTC showing bullish 5/5 signal, ETH showing bearish 4/5 signal ‚Üí Divergent momentum between major crypto assets.

    Pattern 3: Sequential Cascade¶

    Observation:
    MACD signals appear across related symbols in sequence over time rather than simultaneously.

    Interpretation:
    Potential leadership or lagging pattern within sector.

    Example: Tech mega-caps signal first (AAPL, MSFT), then mid-caps signal days later ‚Üí Potential cascade pattern in momentum shifts.

    Pattern 4: Isolated High-Probability Signal¶

    Observation:
    One symbol shows signal with 5/5 confluence while all others remain neutral or show low-confluence signals.

    Interpretation:
    Asset-specific momentum shift with exceptional confluence rather than broad market pattern.

    Example: Single stock shows bullish 5/5 signal while entire sector neutral ‚Üí Isolated high-probability setup characteristics.

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid¶

    Learn from Common Pitfalls

    These mistakes are frequently made by new Augury Grid users. Avoid them to improve your results:

    Mistake #1: Monitoring Too Many Symbols¶

    The Problem: Adding 30-40 symbols to the grid creates information overload. Too many symbols = difficulty tracking opportunities and analysis paralysis.

    The Fix:

    • Start with 8-12 high-quality, liquid symbols you understand well
    • Focus on symbols with strong trends, high volume, and clear technical patterns
    • As you gain experience, gradually expand to 15-20 symbols maximum
    • Quality over quantity: 10 well-analyzed symbols better than 40 ignored ones

    Remember: Successful traders focus deeply on a small watchlist. Start with 8-12 symbols and master them first.

    Mistake #2: Trading Every Grid Signal¶

    The Problem: Not all confluence scores are equal. Trading 2/5 or 3/5 signals reduces win rate. Only the highest-confluence setups (4/5, 5/5) deserve action.

    The Fix:

    • 5/5 confluence: All five systems agree ‚Üí highest priority, strongest setups
    • 4/5 confluence: Four systems agree ‚Üí high quality, worth investigating
    • 3/5 confluence: Moderate agreement ‚Üí proceed with caution, need additional confirmation
    • 2/5 or less: Low agreement ‚Üí skip these signals, wait for better confluence

    Remember: Only trade 4/5 and 5/5 confluence signals. Let 2/5 and 3/5 signals pass‚Äîpatience improves results.

    Mistake #3: Skipping Individual Chart Analysis¶

    The Problem: The grid is a filter, not a final trading decision. Entering trades based solely on grid confluence without reviewing the actual chart leads to poor entries and missed context.

    The Fix:

    • Grid highlights opportunity ‚Üí ALWAYS open individual chart for detailed analysis
    • Check price action: Is price at support/resistance? Clean breakout or choppy range?
    • Review Pentarch signals: When did signal fire? Which signal type (IGN vs BDN)?
    • Confirm with Janus Atlas levels and Volume Oracle before entering

    Remember: Grid = screener, not strategy. Use it to filter your watchlist, then perform full chart analysis before trading.

    Mistake #4: Ignoring the Confluence Score Numbers¶

    The Problem: Treating all green (bullish) or red (bearish) signals equally, regardless of confluence score. A 2/5 bullish signal is far weaker than a 5/5 bullish signal.

    The Fix:

    • Always check the confluence number: 5/5 > 4/5 > 3/5 > 2/5
    • Prioritize your attention: Review 5/5 signals first, then 4/5, then 3/5
    • Set alerts only for 4/5 and 5/5 signals to reduce noise
    • Higher confluence = higher probability, simpler decision-making

    Remember: The number matters more than the color. A 5/5 signal deserves immediate attention; a 2/5 signal can be ignored.

    Mistake #5: Using Wrong Timeframe for Your Trading Style¶

    The Problem: Day traders using Daily charts on the grid, or swing traders using 5-minute charts. Timeframe mismatch leads to irrelevant signals and poor results.

    The Fix:

    • Day traders: Use 5-minute, 15-minute, or 1-hour charts for intraday opportunities
    • Swing traders: Use 4-hour, Daily, or Weekly charts for multi-day holds
    • Position traders: Use Daily or Weekly charts for long-term trends
    • Remember: Grid uses the timeframe of your active chart

    Remember: Match the grid's chart timeframe to your trading style. Day traders need intraday timeframes; swing traders need higher timeframes.

    Mistake #6: Never Updating Your Watchlist¶

    The Problem: Markets change. Symbols that were trending 2 months ago may now be dead/choppy. Static watchlists become less effective over time.

    The Fix:

    • Review watchlist weekly: Remove low-volume, choppy, or stagnant symbols
    • Add new symbols showing strong trends, high relative strength, or sector rotation
    • Rotate symbols based on market conditions (growth stocks in bull markets, defensive in bear markets)
    • Keep watchlist dynamic and relevant to current market environment

    Remember: A fresh, actively managed watchlist performs better than a stale, outdated one. Review and rotate symbols regularly.

    Understanding Reduced Reliability Conditions

    Augury Grid signals show reduced reliability in certain market conditions. Recognizing these environments helps set appropriate expectations:

    Broad Choppy Market Conditions (Market-Wide Indecision)¶

    The Condition: When overall market environment (SPY, QQQ, or sector indices) is sideways/choppy, most stocks/assets in your watchlist exhibit similar indecisive behavior.

    What Happens: Grid shows mostly 2/5 and 3/5 confluence scores across all symbols. Few 4/5 or 5/5 signals appear. Symbols that do signal often flip from Bullish to Neutral to Bearish within 5-10 bars. P&L percentages hover around ¬±1-2%, showing no sustained follow-through. Win rate drops because even "good" setups lack the broader market momentum to follow through.

    Context: Augury Grid analyzes individual symbol momentum via MACD and confluence factors. But individual symbols are influenced by overall market sentiment. During broad market choppy periods (VIX elevated but direction unclear, major indices range-bound), even stocks with strong individual confluence struggle to trend. This isn't Augury failing‚Äîit's accurately showing that momentum exists but lacks supporting market context. Best practice: Check SPY/QQQ regime first using Pentarch or Omnideck. If broader market shows neutral/choppy regime, treat Augury signals with higher skepticism. Trade smaller size or wait for market clarity. When overall market is trending (clear bull or bear regime), Augury signals become significantly more reliable.

    Trading Low Confluence Score Signals (2/5, 3/5)&lgrid-analysis shows various outcomes. Observation suggests higher confluence correlates with more sustained moves, but no threshold guarantees specific results.

    Context: Confluence scores measure how many supporting factors align with the MACD crossover. Higher scores (4/5, 5/5) indicate: price action agrees, volume supports the move, higher timeframe aligns, momentum is strong, and structure confirms direction. Lower scores (2/5, 3/5) mean several factors are missing or conflicting. A 2/5 signal might have MACD crossover + volume, but price action is weak, higher timeframe disagrees, and momentum is shallow. This doesn't mean the 2/5 signal will definitely fail, but historical observation shows fewer 2/5 signals produce sustained follow-through compared to 4/5 signals. Notice in examples throughout this documentation: focus consistently emphasizes 4/5 and 5/5 signals. This isn't arbitrary‚Äîit reflects that higher confluence matters. Best practice: If capital is limited, focus exclusively on 4/5 and 5/5 signals. If monitoring capacity is limited, set alerts only for highest-confluence setups. Think of 2/5 and 3/5 as "educational signals"‚Äîthey show potential, but shouldn't compete for attention with higher-quality 4/5 and 5/5 opportunities.

    Whipsaw & Transition Periods¶

    The Condition: Individual symbols transitioning between regimes‚Äîmoving from downtrend to consolidation to uptrend, or vice versa.

    What Happens: Grid shows signal instability: Symbol X fires Bullish 5/5 ‚Üí 3 bars later flips to Neutral ‚Üí 5 bars later fires Bearish 4/5 ‚Üí then back to Neutral. Confluence scores fluctuate rapidly. P&L shows +3%, -2%, +1%, -1% oscillating pattern. This happens because MACD is centered around zero crossover, and during transitions, the histogram oscillates around the zero line without committing to either direction.

    Context: MACD-based systems (like Augury Grid) excel at identifying momentum within established trends. They struggle during regime transitions when momentum itself is unstable. During these periods, the histogram crosses zero multiple times in both directions as the market "decides" which way to trend. These whipsaws are inherent to zero-crossing methodologies. Best practice: When you see a symbol flip signals 3+ times within 20 bars, that symbol is in transition‚Äîremove it from active consideration temporarily. Revisit it after 10-15 bars to see if regime has stabilized. Or combine with Pentarch: only trade Augury signals when Pentarch regime bars show clear color (green or red, not gray) for the same symbol. This filters out symbols in transition.

    Mismatched Timeframe & Trading Strategy¶

    The Condition: Using Augury Grid on a timeframe that doesn't match your trading style or hold duration.

    What Happens:

    • Day trader using Daily chart grid: Signals fire once every few days. By the time signal appears, much of the intraday move has already occurred. Miss 80% of opportunities waiting for daily signals.
    • Swing trader using 5m chart grid: Signals fire constantly (10+ signals per hour across watchlist). Impossible to act on all of them. Most reverse before you can analyze and enter. Signal overload leads to paralysis or random selection.

    Context: Augury Grid's entire watchlist uses the timeframe of your active chart. This is by design‚Äîit allows rapid scanning of multiple symbols on a consistent timeframe. But it requires matching chart timeframe to trading style. Scalpers (hold minutes-hours) ‚Üí 5m-15m charts. Day traders (hold hours-day) ‚Üí 15m-1H charts. Swing traders (hold days-weeks) ‚Üí 4H-Daily charts. Position traders (hold weeks-months) ‚Üí Daily-Weekly charts. Mismatch creates either: (1) Too few signals (missing opportunities), or (2) Too many signals (overwhelming noise). Best practice: If you're a day trader, don't use Augury Grid on a Daily chart. If you're a swing trader, don't use it on 15m charts. Match the grid's timeframe to your actual hold period.

    Highly Correlated Watchlist (All Symbols Move Together)¶

    The Condition: Watchlist contains symbols that are highly correlated‚Äîthey all move together (e.g., all tech stocks, all crypto, all oil stocks).

    What Happens: Grid shows "diversification illusion." When tech sector rallies, all 30 symbols in watchlist flash Bullish 5/5 simultaneously. Feels like 30 opportunities, but it's really ONE opportunity (tech sector) expressed through 30 symbols. When sector reverses, all 30 flip Bearish together. No true diversification. Risk concentration masked by symbol count.

    Context: Augury Grid's value is finding THE BEST opportunities across a diversified watchlist. If all symbols are correlated, the grid just amplifies sector moves without adding information. A watchlist of AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, META, NVDA, AMD, TSLA (all high-beta tech) will show nearly identical signals. This defeats the purpose of multi-symbol scanning. Better watchlist design: Mix sectors (tech, healthcare, energy, financials, consumer), mix asset classes (stocks, crypto, commodities), mix market caps (large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap). When tech gives Bullish 5/5, healthcare might show Bearish 5/5, and energy shows Neutral. THIS is useful‚Äîit shows WHERE opportunity is rotating. If your grid shows all symbols firing same signal simultaneously, your watchlist is too correlated. Diversify symbol selection.

    Low Liquidity & Thinly Traded Symbols¶

    The Condition: Including penny stocks, micro-cap altcoins, or illiquid futures contracts in your watchlist.

    What Happens: Signals appear (MACD crossover is valid), but price action is erratic. A Bullish 5/5 fires, but the bid-ask spread is 5%. Price gaps intraday due to lack of buyers. Slippage eats most of theoretical profit. P&L shows +8% but actual fill is at +3% due to liquidity issues.

    Context: Augury Grid analyzes momentum mathematically (MACD calculations). It doesn't "know" about liquidity, bid-ask spreads, or average daily volume. A $0.50 penny stock can show perfect 5/5 confluence, but if daily volume is only 10,000 shares, entering a 5,000-share position moves the market against you. MACD confluence works best on liquid instruments where your order doesn't materially impact price. Best practice: Pre-filter your watchlist by liquidity before adding to Augury Grid. For stocks: minimum $5M average daily volume. For crypto: minimum $50M daily volume (on the specific exchange you trade). For futures: use front-month contracts with high open interest. Grid shows technical signals; you must ensure those signals are tradeable with acceptable slippage.

    Using Grid Signals Without Individual Chart Confirmation¶

    The Condition: Trading directly from grid signals without pulling up individual charts to verify setup quality.

    What Happens: Grid shows AAPL Bullish 5/5. You enter immediately. Pull up AAPL chart afterward and realize: it's at major resistance (Janus Atlas Weekly High), volume is below average (Volume Oracle shows this), and it just had 3 failed bullish attempts at this level. The 5/5 signal is technically valid, but context makes it low-probability. Trade fails.

    Context: Augury Grid is a screener‚Äîits job is to surface candidates worth investigating, not to provide complete trade analysis. The grid analyzes MACD confluence across multiple symbols efficiently. But it doesn't check: support/resistance context, volume quality, recent failed attempts, higher timeframe alignment, news events, earnings dates, sector rotation. These require individual chart analysis. Best practice workflow: (1) Grid identifies candidates (4/5 and 5/5 signals), (2) Pull up individual chart for top 3-5 candidates, (3) Check Janus Atlas levels (are we at major support/resistance?), (4) Check Volume Oracle (is volume supporting this move?), (5) Check Pentarch (does regime support this direction?), (6) THEN decide to enter. Grid narrows 40 symbols to 5 candidates. You narrow 5 candidates to 1-2 actual trades. That's the workflow.

    Recognizing Reduced Reliability Environments

    Practical Identification:

    • Entire grid showing mostly 2/5 and 3/5 scores: Broad market chop‚Äîreduce activity or trade higher confluence only
    • SPY/QQQ showing neutral/gray on Pentarch: Market lacks direction‚ÄîAugury signals less reliable

    • P&L oscillating ¬±1-2% without sustained moves: Choppy conditions or wrong timeframe
    • Symbol volume under $5M daily (stocks) or $50M (crypto): Liquidity concerns‚Äîremove from watchlist
    • Trading from grid without checking individual charts: Missing context‚Äîverify setups individually

    Remember: Augury Grid's strength is efficiency‚Äîscanning 40 symbols simultaneously to find momentum shifts. Its weakness is context‚Äîit doesn't know about support/resistance, volume quality, broader market regime, or individual symbol liquidity. The grid finds candidates. You validate candidates. Best results come from combining Augury's scanning efficiency with individual chart analysis using Janus Atlas (levels), Volume Oracle (volume confirmation), and Pentarch (regime context). Grid ‚Üí Candidates ‚Üí Validation ‚Üí Trade. That's the complete workflow.
